About Me

I love problem solving, and I love a good puzzle! I was drawn to physics and mathematics because I really enjoy the problem solving skills, quantitative reasoning, and collaboration fostered in these areas of study.

I'm also very interested in education and education policy. I have had numerous opportunities to both develop my skills in this area and learn more about this issue. I have volunteered with several nonprofits offering educational services including Sparks Within Reach, Math-M-Addicts, and the National Museum of Mathematics. I have also served as teaching intern at my high school, working with students in 9th grade geometry, and volunteer with the Yale Mathcounts Outreach club which works with local New Haven schools. I am also pursuing a certificate in Education Studies.

In my free time I enjoy listening to podcasts, going on walks, and playing the New York Times word games.
